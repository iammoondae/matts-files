# Games Center - Grade 1–2 Game Catalog
## Target Audience: Grades 1–2 (Ages 6–8)

This catalog details the specific educational games designed for Grade 1 and Grade 2 learners. The games focus on fundamental literacy, basic arithmetic, simple sensory observations, and community social studies.

---

## 🎮 1. Sensory Memory Match
* **Subject Focus**: Science (Senses & Anatomy)
* **MATATAG Alignment**: Identify the five sense organs and describe their functions.
* **Gameplay Mechanics**: 
  - Standard card matching layout with a 3x4 grid (12 cards) for Grade 1, and 4x4 grid (16 cards) for Grade 2.
  - Players flip cards to find matching pairs matching a sense organ to its qualitative function.
* **Match Examples**:
  - `👁️ Eye` matches `🌈 Seeing a Rainbow`
  - `👂 Ear` matches `🔔 Hearing a Bell`
  - `👃 Nose` matches `👃 Smelling a Rose`
  - `👅 Tongue` matches `🍯 Tasting Sweet Honey`
  - `👋 Skin` matches `❄️ Feeling Cold Ice`
* **Visual Style**: Sleek card flip animations, clean illustrations of organs, and vibrant colored borders.
* **XP Reward**: 30 XP
* **Coin Reward**: 5 Coins

## 🔢 2. Fruit Counter & Sorter
* **Subject Focus**: Mathematics (Numeracy & Counting)
* **MATATAG Alignment**: Read and write whole numbers up to 100 (Grade 1) and up to 1,000 (Grade 2).
* **Gameplay Mechanics**:
  - A grocery stand is filled with mixed fruits (apples, oranges, bananas).
  - The owl mascot prompts the learner: *"Can you place exactly 7 apples in the basket?"*
  - The player drags and drops fruits from the counter into the basket.
  - Grade 2 increases difficulty by introducing place value boxes (Hundreds, Tens, Ones buckets) to sort blocks.
* **Difficulty Progression**:
  - **Level 1 (Grade 1)**: Single digit numbers (1–9).
  - **Level 2 (Grade 1)**: Double digit numbers (10–20).
  - **Level 3 (Grade 2)**: Grouping objects into tens bundles and ones.
* **XP Reward**: 40 XP
* **Coin Reward**: 8 Coins

## ✍️ 3. Phonics Word Builder
* **Subject Focus**: English / Filipino (Literacy)
* **MATATAG Alignment**: Match letters with their sounds; spell simple CVC (Consonant-Vowel-Consonant) words.
* **Gameplay Mechanics**:
  - An image is displayed (e.g., a cat, a dog, a sun, or "aso", "pusa", "araw").
  - An incomplete word block is shown underneath (e.g., `C - _ - T` or `A - S - _`).
  - Scrambled letter bubbles float at the bottom.
  - The player drags the correct letter bubble into the blank slot.
* **Match Examples**:
  - Picture of a **sun** -> Drag `U` to complete `S - U - N`
  - Picture of a **pusa** -> Drag `P` to complete `P - U - S - A`
* **Visual Style**: Soft pastel letter bubbles, clean vector cartoon illustrations, and bouncy gravity physics.
* **XP Reward**: 35 XP
* **Coin Reward**: 6 Coins

## 🌦️ 4. Weather Dress-Up (Tobi's Weather)
* **Subject Focus**: Science / Makabansa (Geography & Daily Life)
* **MATATAG Alignment**: Identify different types of weather; express how weather affects clothing and activities.
* **Gameplay Mechanics**:
  - A window displays the active weather (rainy, sunny, windy, stormy).
  - The owl mascot Tobi stands shivering or sweating.
  - A closet opens with items of clothing (raincoat, sunglasses, umbrella, sweater, t-shirt, boots).
  - The child drags appropriate clothes onto Tobi to prepare him for the day.
* **Scenarios**:
  - **Sunny Day**: Sunglasses, Cap, T-shirt.
  - **Rainy Day**: Raincoat, Boots, Umbrella.
  - **Cold/Windy Day**: Sweater, Scarf, Long pants.
* **XP Reward**: 30 XP
* **Coin Reward**: 5 Coins

## 🏘️ 5. Community Helpers Matching
* **Subject Focus**: Makabansa (Civics & Local Community)
* **MATATAG Alignment**: Identify the roles and contributions of community helpers.
* **Gameplay Mechanics**:
  - Split layout: 3 community workers on the left, 3 tools or workplaces on the right.
  - The player draws a matching line from the helper to their workplace/tool.
* **Match Examples**:
  - `🧑‍🏫 Teacher` -> `🏫 Classroom`
  - `🧑‍⚕️ Doctor` -> `🩺 Stethoscope`
  - `🧑‍🚒 Firefighter` -> `🚒 Fire Truck`
  - `🧑‍🌾 Farmer` -> `🌾 Rice Field`
* **XP Reward**: 35 XP
* **Coin Reward**: 6 Coins
