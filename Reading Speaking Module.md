# Grades 1–6 Reading and Speaking Module Framework

## I. Phonological Awareness & Phonics (Foundational Skills)

### Phonemic Awareness
**Definition/Description:** The ability to hear, identify, isolate, blend, segment, add, delete, and substitute individual sounds (phonemes) in spoken words.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Recognizes individual sounds in words.
- Manipulates sounds to create new words.
- Demonstrates readiness for decoding and spelling.

**Sample Activities:**
- Sound matching games
- Rhyming circles
- Phoneme substitution challenges
- Oral blending activities

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Correctly identifies beginning, middle, and ending sounds.
- Segments words into phonemes with at least 80% accuracy.
- Successfully substitutes phonemes to create new words.

---

### Blending Sounds
**Definition/Description:** Combining individual phonemes to form recognizable spoken or written words.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Blends sounds accurately to read words.
- Uses blending strategies when decoding unfamiliar words.

**Sample Activities:**
- Teacher-led oral blending
- Sound-to-word races
- Decodable word reading

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Accurately blends phonemes into words.
- Reads grade-level decodable words independently.

---

### Segmenting Words into Individual Sounds
**Definition/Description:** Breaking spoken words into separate phonemes.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Identifies all sounds in simple and complex words.
- Uses segmentation for spelling and decoding.

**Sample Activities:**
- Elkonin boxes
- Sound tapping
- Phoneme counting exercises

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Correctly segments words into phonemes.
- Demonstrates phoneme awareness during spelling tasks.

---

### Letter–Sound Correspondence
**Definition/Description:** Understanding the relationship between letters and their associated sounds.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Matches letters and letter combinations to sounds.
- Applies sound-symbol knowledge while reading.

**Sample Activities:**
- Alphabet drills
- Letter-sound matching cards
- Interactive phonics games

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Correctly identifies letter sounds.
- Uses sound-symbol knowledge when decoding words.

---

### Consonant Blends and Digraphs
**Definition/Description:** Recognition and application of common consonant blends and digraphs.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Reads words containing blends and digraphs accurately.

**Sample Activities:**
- Blend sorting
- Digraph word hunts
- Reading passages with target patterns

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Reads blend and digraph words accurately in context.

---

### Vowel Patterns
**Definition/Description:** Understanding common vowel spelling patterns and pronunciation rules.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Identifies and applies common vowel patterns in reading.

**Sample Activities:**
- Word family sorting
- Vowel pattern games
- Decodable texts

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Correctly decodes words containing taught vowel patterns.

---

### R-Controlled Vowels
**Definition/Description:** Recognizing vowel sounds influenced by the letter r.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Reads and spells r-controlled vowel words accurately.

**Sample Activities:**
- Word sorting
- Reading practice
- Vocabulary building exercises

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Correctly identifies and reads r-controlled vowel words.

---

### Multisyllabic Word Decoding
**Definition/Description:** Applying decoding strategies to words containing multiple syllables.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Breaks longer words into manageable syllables.
- Reads complex words accurately.

**Sample Activities:**
- Syllable division practice
- Word chunking activities
- Morphology exploration

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Correctly decodes multisyllabic words in grade-level texts.

---

### Irregularly Spelled Words
**Definition/Description:** Recognition of high-frequency words that do not follow common phonics rules.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Reads common exception words automatically.

**Sample Activities:**
- Flashcards
- Word walls
- Repeated reading

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Reads irregular words accurately and automatically.

---

### Syllabication Patterns
**Definition/Description:** Understanding rules and patterns for dividing words into syllables.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Applies syllabication strategies when decoding unfamiliar words.

**Sample Activities:**
- Syllable clapping
- Syllable sorting
- Word analysis

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Correctly identifies syllable boundaries in unfamiliar words.

---

## II. Word Recognition & Vocabulary

### High-Frequency / Sight Words
**Definition/Description:** Frequently occurring words recognized instantly without decoding.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Reads sight words automatically and accurately.

**Sample Activities:**
- Flashcard drills
- Timed recognition games
- Sight word bingo

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Reads grade-level sight words with automaticity.

---

### Decodable Words by Grade Level
**Definition/Description:** Words that follow taught phonics patterns and can be sounded out.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Applies phonics skills to decode unfamiliar words.

**Sample Activities:**
- Decodable readers
- Word-building exercises

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Accurately reads grade-level decodable words.

---

### Morphology
**Definition/Description:** Study of meaningful word parts including roots, prefixes, and suffixes.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Uses morphology to determine word meanings.

**Sample Activities:**
- Word dissection
- Affix matching
- Root word trees

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Correctly identifies roots and affixes.
- Explains meaning changes caused by affixes.

---

### Compound Words and Contractions
**Definition/Description:** Understanding how words combine or shorten to form new meanings.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Recognizes and uses compound words and contractions appropriately.

**Sample Activities:**
- Matching games
- Sentence construction

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Correctly identifies and uses compound words and contractions.

---

### Word Relationships
**Definition/Description:** Understanding synonyms, antonyms, homonyms, and shades of meaning.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Selects appropriate words based on meaning and context.

**Sample Activities:**
- Vocabulary maps
- Matching tasks

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Correctly identifies word relationships.

---

### Context Clues
**Definition/Description:** Using surrounding text to infer word meanings.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Determines unfamiliar word meanings independently.

**Sample Activities:**
- Cloze passages
- Vocabulary detective activities

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Correctly infers meanings from context.

---

### Academic Vocabulary
**Definition/Description:** High-utility words used across academic disciplines.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Uses academic language accurately in speech and reading.

**Sample Activities:**
- Vocabulary journals
- Academic discussion prompts

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Appropriately uses target academic vocabulary.

---

### Figurative Language
**Definition/Description:** Understanding language used beyond literal meaning.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Identifies and interprets figurative language.

**Sample Activities:**
- Figurative language scavenger hunts
- Poetry exploration

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Explains meanings of similes, metaphors, and personification.

---

### Word Analysis Strategies
**Definition/Description:** Applying structural and contextual strategies to understand words.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Independently analyzes unfamiliar words.

**Sample Activities:**
- Word-solving workshops
- Morphology tasks

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Demonstrates effective use of analysis strategies.

---

## III. Reading Fluency

### Accurate Word Recognition
**Definition/Description:** Reading words correctly and consistently.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Reads with high accuracy.

**Sample Activities:**
- Guided reading
- Partner reading

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Maintains at least 95% accuracy in grade-level texts.

---

### Appropriate Reading Rate
**Definition/Description:** Reading at a speed that supports comprehension.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Reads smoothly without excessive hesitation.

**Sample Activities:**
- Timed repeated readings
- Fluency practice passages

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Meets grade-level fluency benchmarks.

---

### Prosody
**Definition/Description:** Reading with expression, phrasing, and appropriate intonation.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Reads naturally and expressively.

**Sample Activities:**
- Reader's theater
- Echo reading

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Demonstrates appropriate expression and phrasing.

---

### Self-Monitoring and Self-Correction
**Definition/Description:** Recognizing and correcting reading errors independently.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Detects and fixes errors while reading.

**Sample Activities:**
- Think-alouds
- Error analysis exercises

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Self-corrects reading mistakes appropriately.

---

### Automaticity in Word Reading
**Definition/Description:** Fast and effortless recognition of familiar words.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Frees cognitive resources for comprehension.

**Sample Activities:**
- Repeated reading
- High-frequency word drills

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Demonstrates rapid recognition of familiar words.

---

## IV. Reading Comprehension – Literature

### Key Ideas and Details
**Definition/Description:** Understanding essential information in literary texts.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Identifies important story elements.

**Sample Activities:**
- Story mapping
- Comprehension questions

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Answers literal comprehension questions accurately.

---

### Central Message / Theme
**Definition/Description:** Determining the lesson or message of a text.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Identifies themes supported by textual evidence.

**Sample Activities:**
- Theme discussions
- Theme charts

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Explains themes using evidence.

---

### Character Analysis
**Definition/Description:** Examining character traits, motivations, and development.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Analyzes character actions and decisions.

**Sample Activities:**
- Character profiles
- Role play

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Supports character analysis with evidence.

---

### Setting and Plot Sequence
**Definition/Description:** Understanding where, when, and how story events unfold.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Identifies setting and sequence of events.

**Sample Activities:**
- Story timelines
- Plot diagrams

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Correctly sequences events.

---

### Cause and Effect
**Definition/Description:** Recognizing relationships between events.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Explains causes and outcomes.

**Sample Activities:**
- Cause-effect charts

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Identifies causes and effects accurately.

---

### Comparing Texts
**Definition/Description:** Identifying similarities and differences among texts.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Compares themes, structures, and perspectives.

**Sample Activities:**
- Venn diagrams

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Makes supported comparisons.

---

### Visual and Multimedia Elements
**Definition/Description:** Interpreting illustrations, graphics, and multimedia features.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Uses visuals to enhance comprehension.

**Sample Activities:**
- Picture analysis
- Video-text comparison

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Explains how visuals support meaning.

---

### Author's Purpose and Point of View
**Definition/Description:** Understanding why a text was written and from whose perspective.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Identifies purpose and viewpoint.

**Sample Activities:**
- Perspective discussions

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Explains purpose and perspective using evidence.

---

### Inference and Evidence-Based Reasoning
**Definition/Description:** Drawing conclusions based on textual clues and prior knowledge.

**Expected Student Outcome:**
- Makes logical inferences supported by evidence.

**Sample Activities:**
- Evidence hunts
- Inferencing tasks

**Assessment Indicators:**
- Supports inferences with textual evidence.
